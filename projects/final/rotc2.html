The Army Reserve Officer Training Corps is divided into two courses, the basic course and the advanced course. Freshman and Sophomore year make up the basic course and Junior and Senior year make up the advanced course. Military Science or MS is class and lab requirement every year and defines what year you're in (MS1 = Freshman, MS2 = Sophomore, etc).

During the MS I year, cadets learn:

  • Basic Land Navigation Skills
  • Squad Positions
  • Drill and Ceremony
  • Customs and Courtesies

During the MS II year, cadets learn:

  • How to Navigate with a compass to points in a grid
  • Team leader positions in a squad
  • Rappelling

During the MS III year, cadets learn and are assessed on:

  • Their ability to lead a squad in tactical training
  • How to manage people and schedules
  • Their ability to navigate with a map, compass, and protractor at night

During the MS IV year, cadets learn:

  • The roles as a platoon leader in the Army
  • How to manage the MS Is, IIs, and IIIs in the Battalion
  • The responsiblities and expectations of officers in the Army
